Malaysian Agro-Science Scenario

Optimizing Paddy Fertilization via Derivatives $f'(t)$

Malaysia is pushing to secure its domestic rice supply chains (aiming for 100% Self-Sufficiency Level). To maximize yields of high-yielding rice varieties like MR297 (developed by MARDI), precision fertilizer application is vital.

Rather than applying fertilizer randomly, we track the crop's Dry Matter Accumulation $f(t)$ in grams per square meter ($\text{g/m}^2$). The first derivative, $f'(t)$, reveals the crop's daily nutrient uptake velocity. Applying nitrogen top-dressing at the exact peak of this derivative (the inflection point) maximizes nutrient uptake efficiency, saves cost, and limits environmental runoff.

The Bio-Calculus Model

Cumulative Biomass $f(t)$
f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}
Predicts cumulative plant biomass ($\text{g/m}^2$) at day $t$.
Daily Growth Rate $f'(t)$ (First Derivative)
f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t - t_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)})^2}
Calculates exact daily growth velocity ($\text{g/m}^2/\text{day}$).
